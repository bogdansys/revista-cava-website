Limbi Straine

Merry Covid Christmas

It’s been almost two years since humanity has had to deal with the massive health crisis brought over by Covid-19. It was first discovered in Wuhan, China at the end of 2019, hence the name, but the exact cause still remains unclear. After that, it was slowly transmitted to the rest of the country. It reached Romania somewhere in February of the year 2020. The official lockdown occurred in March, when everyone underestimated just how serious the situation would get. Soon enough, we were very well familiarized with the social distancing and the mandatory wear of masks, not to mention the times when we were not allowed to leave our houses. The new generations had never experienced anything like this, which is why the situation still seemed like a science fiction movie to some. In the summer of 2020, just when we thought the number of cases would drop, we were hit by a new wave a few months later. Then another wave hit, followed by another. This year, in 2021, we are expecting the 5th wave somewhere at the end and the beginning of 2022. As we learned and adapted, by developing a vaccine, which faced a lot of controversy from the public, but also proved to be effective, the virus changed too. The Delta variant is said to affect people of younger ages. From a virus known as SARS-CoV-2, which targeted mostly old and sick people, we now have one which can attack minors. New laws were implemented, such as the need for the green pass in many public locations, but also for travelling and recently for going to work. Hundreds of campaigns encouraging people to get the vaccine were made, all in hopes of eradicating this horrible virus. In Romania, for example, only 42.8% of the adults are vaccinated, while the highest number is held by Ireland with 92.6%. The huge difference between the countries goes to show the mindset of the people, but also the level of restrictions. We have yet to impose the necessity of the green pass for attending your job, as have other countries. However, as news of this law in the making, a high number of people going in for the first dosage was recorded, proving that with the right consequence, the population will slowly, but surely adhere to the requirements.

In Romania, the first deaths occurred on the 23rd of March 2020, while the first casualty worldwide was recorded on the 11th of January 2020, which would mark the start of the battle against the virus, which we are still fighting today. In total, from the outbreak of the virus, Covid has killed 5.18 million people. It is safe to say that some aspects of life will never be the same. Many students entering high school never got to properly meet their colleagues, while others started college online. Many lost their jobs, due to some companies going bankrupt. Still, many new businesses took off in this pandemic, the online shopping being the most prolific. Even though, we have found a vaccine and UK has already approved the first anti-Covid pill, many people are still dying in the overpacked hospitals and the need for supplies is a never-ending crisis. Not all civilized countries have adapted the same. In Romania, for example, there are not enough hospital beds for people who need intensive care, so they are put in improvised beds and chairs on the hallways. The medical staff, can barely keep up with the constant ambulances arriving at the hospitals every few minutes. Many may not realize just how overly solicited and overworked the doctors have been this past year. For many, a 14 hours shift is the normality, while others can spend even two days without sleeping.

The medical field was not the only sector financially affected by this virus. As a result of the funds redirected towards hospitals and the research necessary for discovering the vaccine, as well as mass producing it, not to mention the medical tools and supplies needed, we are now faced with inflation worldwide. Adding to that, climate change and pollution have also made their presence felt, the costs of electricity, gas and water doubling in price. The inflation we are faced with this years is said to be the highest in the last 31 years. The highest rate is currently held by the US, with consumer prices jumping 6.2% in October. The main components of the recent inflation are: Services (418.3%), Non-energy Industrial goods (269.1%), Food (217.6%) and Energy (95%).

Contrary to popular belief, climate change, as well as other world problems have existed long before the appearance of Covid-19. However, their urgency became less important as the entirety of the planet was fighting to find a cure for the virus. Now the only issue is getting more and more people to get vaccinated, meaning that the current situation has allowed for past climate projects to be resumed and new measured to be taken. Recently, the environmental problems have clashed with the ongoing fight against the virus, as well as the worldwide economic crisis, which has resulted in the current inflation.

In conclusion, looking back to everything that has happened, humanity has come a long way, from facing a new virus to finding a vaccine for it, while also creating new laws to reinforce the administration of the vaccine and maintain contagious-free conditions in workspaces, as well as in public. All of our progress, as a whole, goes to show what setting rivalries aside and working together can accomplish, as well as how much technology has helped us in the medical field. We can never be certain of how long this pandemic will last, but it is important to inform the public that change is possible if we just follow some rules and try to act as a community in our favor.
