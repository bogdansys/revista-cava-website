Critica

Red (Taylor's Version)

If you've met me, you already know Taylor Swift's discography is the soundtrack of my life. After her masters controversy, I thought my favorite album was lost for good; taxi cabs and busy streets, they'd never bring it back to me... Until it was rerecorded and rereleased, with Taylor Swift's full ownership. Two weeks later, I'm finally ready to talk about the most important event of November: the release of "Red (Taylor's Version)".

"Masters controversy?" Let me explain. In 2005, when Taylor Swift was 16 years old and at the very beginning of her career as a singer-songwriter, she signed a record deal with Big Machine Records, which involved their ownership of the masters to her first six albums. She tried to buy them back after she ended her contract, but to no avail, and when Scooter Braun bought Big Machine Records, her masters were part of the purchase. In an unprecedented move for such a mainstream artist, Taylor Swift decided to rerecord her first six albums and put them out the way she'd always wanted to, but wasn't allowed - different production and features, as well as a longer track list, complete with most of the songs she'd originally written for the album that weren't released.

Now, what makes "Red" so special? "Red" is the first Taylor Swift album I listened to in its entirety, the only one I have a physical copy of, and it's the era that introduced me to her music back in third grade - though I have to say, "I Knew You Were Trouble" hits a little bit different now. "Red" also perfectly documents Taylor's transition from country to pop - its soft guitar and beautifully crafted lyrics make it sound like nothing new, when it's actually the first time she seriously experiments with her sound, resulting in some of her most iconic songs to date.

...and when "Red (Taylor's Version)" began again on 12 November, it only reinforced its status as a a fan favorite and a Taylor Swift classic. 30 songs and 2 hours long, it starts off with "State of Grace", an album opener just as good, if not better than "...Ready For It?" ("Reputation") and "Tim McGraw" ("Taylor Swift"). The vocals are more mature, the production sharper and the autumn air crisper than ever, and everything is put together so well I almost forget to skip the happy songs.

That being said, the 9 previously unreleased songs Taylor added to the track list are what truly completes the "Red" experience. From the heart-numbing "Nothing New" to the pure pop "Message In A Bottle", these tracks prove the timelessness of Taylor's songwriting; it transcends personal experience to the point where every listener is woven into the same narrative and gets to live through every emotion along with Taylor. Not only that, but they paint Taylor as the truly complex person she really is, much more than the boy-crazy perpetually heartbroken persona the media and apparently her own label pushed onto her. All the so-called vault tracks ("From The Vault") go hard, but the cherry on top of this red velvet cake is definitely "All Too Well (10 Minute Version) (Taylor's Version) (From The Vault)" (Yeah, it really rolls off the tongue).

So what's up with "All Too Well" (ATW) and all the parentheses? "Red" was mostly written about Taylor's relationship with Jake Gyllenhall, a relationship as tumultuous as it was short, due to Taylor and Jake's age difference, and Jake's alleged toxic behaviour, and ATW was written in the midst of their break-up. Full of beautiful imagery and raw emotion, ATW became a defining track for "Red", all powered by the fans; it wasn't a single, nor was it especially promoted when the album was first released. Later, the news broke out that Taylor had had to shorten it for the album - from a version that was 10 full minutes long. This long version serves as a perfect close to the album, incorporating everything we love about it into 10 minutes of pure heartbreak, the kind only Taylor Swift can put into music.

People say there's a Taylor Swift song for everything, and they're right; "Red" is best described by one of its own tracks - it's sad, beautiful, tragic, and Taylor's Version is a 10/10. Stream "Red (Taylor's Version)", but be warned, I bet you'll be thinking about it for a while...

P.S.: All due respect to ELVIRA, but what was "Girl At Home (Taylor's Version)"?


articol scris de Ilinca Sotirescu
