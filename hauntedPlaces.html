Top 5 most haunted places in Romania

Halloween is a world known celebration where people dress up as scary characters or real life people, decorate their houses and carve out pumpkins. However, many forget the roots of this holiday, as the initially frightening and sinister costumes have been wrapped in advertising campaigns and children stories, now not having such a grotesque look. In Romania this celebration didn’t initially exist, but was later adopted from the Americans. However, that does not mean that haunted and bizarre places have not existed in this country for a long time. Apart from witches, who are still present today and the well-known myths of vampires and other creatures, Romania has quite a lot of haunted locations, some which are not known even by the locals. Therefore, in this article you will find 5 locations amongst the most haunted in this country.

5. The Witches’ Pond

Near Bucharest, close to Cernica forest, there is a pond which has been known for the legends wrapped around it. It is said that weird phenomenon happen near this patch of water. Hidden in the very middle of the forest, the 5 diameter pond appears to be nothing out of the ordinary. However, many roma witches gather here every year with the occasion of St. George, Sanziene and St. Andrew. It is said that in these nights, gates towards other worlds open and any spell or curse will come true. Even for those who refuse to believe in this legend, the place itself is still sinister. Animals refuse to drink water from the pond, no matter how thirsty they are and there is no presence of life in the water. Strange weather has also been spotted by the locals, with globular thunders or out-of-the-blue storms.

4. Cișmigiu Hotel

Named initially Palace Hotel, it was built in 1912 and is situated on the Queen Elizabet Boulevard. The building is considered a historical monument. However, if you look it up online, you will probably raise an eyebrow seeing that you can book a room right now. The truth is, not every haunted place needs to be abandoned and creepy. This specific location is a luxury building with a hidden dark past. In the communist era, the hotel was disregarded by the authorities, slowly degrading with time. In 1990 the building was transformed into a student dorm for the Theater and Film Academy. In this time period, a girl named Neli Bejan fell from the 2nd floor into the elevator shaft. She stood there and called for help for about three hours, but to no avail. Some people have said that they can sometimes still hear her screams of terror, which is why this hotel is quite popular amongst tourists and Romanian citizens alike.

3. Chiajna Monastery

It is a monastery located on the outskirts of Bucharest, surrounded by legends and beliefs, the most prominent one being that it is cursed. This building represents a national heritage site, being protected by the Information Centre in Giulesti Park. The construction of this church began somewhere in the late 18th century during the reign of Alexander Ypsilanti (1774-1782). However, before even being finished, the church was bombarded by the Turks, who believed it to be a military objective. Everything inside the building burned, but it remained standing, as it can be seen today. Its great bell was cast into Dambovita River and the locals have said that it can still be heard on full moon nights, despite the place being deserted. In addition to that, many disappearances have been reported in the monastery, especially of Romani people. The place was also witness to two murders, one before and one after 1990. Locals say that it is the spirits of those people who ring the bell.

2. House of the Devil

Located on General Propangeresu Street, the mysterious appearance is given by the abundance of ivy which is covering it. Legend has it that evil roams around this place. It is said that in the interwar period, three women did here, two of which were killed and the third committed suicide. The ivy almost completely concealing the existence of this house is said to be holding the three souls captive of the women who were violently brought to the afterlife. It is also said that one of the walls, the satanic number 666 can be seen. Few people have managed to break into this building, as there is no actual entryway and they reported feeling a strange presence around them.

1. Hoia Baciu Forest

Located in the depths of Transylvania, just outside of Cluj-Napoca, this forest is known as World’s Most Haunted Place. Named after a shepherd who went missing with a flock of 200 sheep, Hoia Baciu came to international attention in 1968 when Emil Barnea, a military technician, photographed what he claimed was a UFO hovering over The Clearing (the most sinister place in the forest). However, what sets this story apart from any other UFO report is the fact that Emil had nothing to gain from it, but everything to lose. As a result, the Communist government believed him to be mad and he lost his job. The people who visit the forest today report having strange symptoms such as nausea, anxiety, the feeling of being watched and electronic devices don’t function very well in this forest. “Ectoplasms” are a common sight for those who are brave enough to enter and they represent trees with an abnormal growth pattern. They usually grow in zig zags or twist spirally. What’s strange is that the spiraling trees do it in a clockwise direction. Other myths include the disappearance of a 5 year old girl who was said to emerge out of the forest years later, unchanged and in the same clothes. Some tourists who have camped there overnight said they were woken up by huff noises from a horse or deer, who would stop once they went to investigate. All the locals fear this forest and few actually venture into it.

To the surprise of many people, Romania is a well-known location for haunted places. The number is much larger than one would think, with a surprising amount of sinister buildings in the very capital of this country.
